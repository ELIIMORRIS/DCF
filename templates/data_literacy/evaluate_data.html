I can extract and evaluate information from tables and graphs to answer questions.

Understanding Data

Accurate data is essential. Real-world data errors and their consequences.
Identify data types
Data can be categorical or numerical.
Elements in graphs and tables:
information from titles, labels, units and legends/keys.

Extracting Information

From tables, examine headers for ach column and row.
From graphs, identify details from x-axis and y-axis to understand what is being measured.
Identify trends over time, highest and lowest values, and comparisons between groups.
Identify outliers or irregulairites, that can lead to interesting insifhts or require futher investigation.

Evaluating Information

Interpret trends, use specific data point to answer questions, evaluate reliability considering the source and context of the data.

Evaluating Data Sets

Encourage students to evaluate small data sets, such as survey results. Introduce questions like:

  • What does the data show?
  • Are there any patterns or trends?
  • What could the data mean for our community?

Activity Idea: Conduct a class survey on favorite school subjects, collect the results in a table, and discuss the findings together.

Activities

Present a data set with intentional errors or irrelevant entries, the task is to identy these issues.
