Learners will understand how to use tables and graphs to understand data.

Understanding Tables and Graphs

Learn the components of graphs and tables and how to extract basic information.

  • Recognise components like titles, axes, labels, units, scales, and legends/keys.
  • Understand how each element provides insights into the data presented.

Develop skills to extract meaningful insights from tables and graphs

  • From tables, examine headers for columns and rows.
  • From graphs, identify details from the x-axis and y-axis, to understand what is being measured.
  • Identify trends over time, highest and lowest values, and comparisons between groups.
  • Identify outliers or irregularities, that can lead to interesting insights or require futher investigation. Recognise how anomalies affect the mean.
Activity: Show a simple bar graph and ask students to identify the title, axes, and labels.
Ask questions like:
  • What does this graph show?
  • What is the most popular value?
  • How many responses were given in total?
