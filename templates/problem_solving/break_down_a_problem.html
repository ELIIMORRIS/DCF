I can break down a problem to predict its outcome.

Decomposition is the process of breaking down a complex problem into smaller, manageable tasks. It helps us solve problems step by step.

Example: Clean a Bedroom

The goal is to clean a bedroom. Let’s break down this process into tasks.
1. Ask students to design their room and identify variables e.g. bed, carpet, wardrobe.
2. Now students can assign functions in the form of cleaning tasks.
Tasks could include hoovering, making a bed, putting away clothes.
3. Identify any patterns, such as tasks that follow a sequence or occur simultaneously.
This is how we can decompose a large task into smaller parts.

This activity has shown by dividing a larger problem into smaller, manageable parts, we can tackle each piece individually and understand how each component contributes to the overall solution.
